What is the CAC Certification?
The
Certified Addiction Counselor (CAC)
credential is designed for individuals beginning their career in addiction counseling. It validates foundational knowledge in substance use disorders, behavioral sciences, and practical counseling experience. Earning the CAC demonstrates your readiness to provide direct care and support to individuals struggling with addiction, under recognized professional standards.
Start your journey as a certified addiction counselor. We'll guide you through the full process:
Requirements – High school diploma or GED, 200-250 hours of addiction and behavioral sciences education, and 2 years or 4,000 hours of work experience.

Advanced Alcohol and Drug Counselor (AADC)
What is the AADC Certification?
The
Advanced Alcohol and Drug Counselor (AADC)
certification is the highest-level credential for licensed professionals with a master’s degree working in the addiction field. It signifies advanced clinical expertise in diagnosis, treatment planning, and therapeutic intervention for individuals with substance use disorders. The AADC is ideal for those in supervisory, clinical, or leadership roles and is widely recognized across IC&RC member boards for its rigor and professional credibility.

Certified Clinical Supervisor (CCS)
What is the CCS Certification?
The
Certified Clinical Supervisor (CCS)
credential is designed for experienced addiction professionals who provide oversight, mentorship, and clinical guidance to other counselors. This advanced certification validates high-level competence in supervision, leadership, and program development. With significant experience in both counseling and supervisory roles, CCS-certified professionals ensure quality care, ethical practice, and workforce development within behavioral health settings.
Start your journey as a certified addiction counselor. We'll guide you through the full process:
Requirements – Current credential as an AODA counselor at the reciprocal level or higher, 10,000 hours of counseling experience (adjusted based on degree level), 4,000 hours of clinical supervisory experience, and 30 hours of education specific to clinical supervision.
Application Process – Fill out the application form, upload required documents, and pay the fee.

Who should apply for initial certification?
ABCAC certifies addiction professionals in Arizona using internationally recognized standards from the IC&RC. We help individuals gain and maintain professional credentials for counseling, peer support, supervision, and more.
What is the cost for initial certification?
The cost for initial certification through ABCAC is $150, which covers application processing and two years of certification (exam fee not included).
What are the requirements to apply?
The requirements to apply for initial certification through the Arizona Board for the Certification of Addiction Counselors (ABCAC) depend on the specific credential, but the general requirements include:
✅ General Requirements (All Applicants):
Residency: Must live or work in Arizona at least 51% of the time at the time of application.
Completed Application: Submit both the General Application Manual and the appropriate Supplemental Manual for your credential.
Education: Varies by credential (see below). All education must be documented with certificates or transcripts.
Supervised Work Experience: Hours must meet credential-specific requirements and be verified by a supervisor.
Signed Code of Ethics: Must agree to and sign ABCAC’s Code of Ethical Conduct.
Payment: $150 initial certification fee (does not include the IC&RC exam fee).
IC&RC Exam: You must pass the required IC&RC examination for your credential.
📚 Credential-Specific Education & Experience Requirements:
Credential	Education	Supervised Experience
CAC	High School Diploma or GED + 200 hrs training	4,000 hrs (2 years)
CADAC	Bachelor's + 200 hrs (90 in addiction, 90 in counseling)	6,000 hrs (3 years)
AADC	Master’s + 180 hrs addiction-specific	2,000 hrs
CPRS	High School Diploma or GED + 46 hrs domain-specific training	500 hrs practical + 25 hrs supervision
CCS	Master’s or qualifying credential + 30 hrs supervision training	10,000 hrs counseling + 4,000 hrs supervision
CPS	Education in prevention domains	Credential-specific
CCJP	Behavioral health background	Experience in criminal justice addictions counseling
📩 For help determining which credential you qualify for, contact abcac@abcac.org or call 480-980-1770.

Is the IC&RC exam included in the $375 fee?
Yes, the $375 fee does include the IC&RC exam — it covers both your certification processing and your IC&RC testing fee, along with two years of certification once approved.
How do I submit my application?
Start by choosing your credential type (e.g., CAC, AADC, CPRS) and reviewing the requirements. Then submit your application and supporting documents through our secure portal. ABCAC will review your materials and guide you through the next steps toward certification. We’re here to help every step of the way.
How long does the certification process take?
Processing typically takes 4 to 6 weeks once all documentation is received. You will be notified of exam registration and next steps by email.
